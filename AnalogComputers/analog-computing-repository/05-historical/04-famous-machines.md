# Famous Analog Computers

## Overview

Throughout history, several analog computers achieved fame for their groundbreaking capabilities, innovative designs, or critical contributions to science and engineering[^1].

## Pre-Electronic Era

### Antikythera Mechanism (~100 BCE)

**Origin:** Ancient Greece, recovered from shipwreck in 1901

**Features:**
- 30+ bronze gears
- Differential gear mechanism
- Multiple dials and pointers

**Computed:**
- Positions of Sun and Moon
- Lunar phases
- Eclipse predictions
- Olympic games calendar
- Planetary positions

**Significance:** Oldest known complex computing device, demonstrates sophisticated ancient technology.

### Kelvin's Tide Predictor (1872-1876)

**Creator:** Sir William Thomson (Lord Kelvin)

**Purpose:** Predict ocean tides

**Mechanism:**
- Pulley system with weighted cords
- Each pulley represents one tidal constituent
- Summed output gives tide height

**Capability:** Up to 20 harmonic components (later versions: 40)

**Impact:** Used worldwide for tide tables until 1960s

### Bush Differential Analyzer (1931)

**Creator:** Vannevar Bush at MIT

**Features:**
- 6 wheel-and-disk integrators
- Torque amplifiers (key innovation)
- Mechanical interconnection shafts
- Input/output tables

**Capabilities:**
- 6th-order differential equations
- Accuracy: ~0.1%

**Impact:**
- Solved previously intractable problems
- Inspired electronic analog computers
- Influenced ENIAC design

**Replicas:** Built at Manchester, Cambridge, Oslo, and other institutions

## Military Analog Computers

### M-9 Gun Director (1942)

**Purpose:** Anti-aircraft fire control

**Developer:** Bell Laboratories

**Features:**
- First electronic analog computer
- Vacuum tube operational amplifiers
- Real-time tracking and prediction
- Automatic gun laying

**Performance:**
- Dramatically improved anti-aircraft accuracy
- 90% of V-1 flying bombs destroyed

### Ford Mark 37 Fire Control System (1944)

**Application:** Naval gunfire control

**Capabilities:**
- Target tracking
- Ballistic computation
- Ship motion compensation
- Multiple target handling

**Components:**
- Mechanical cams and gears
- Synchros and servos
- Electromechanical integration

### Norden Bombsight (1943)

**Purpose:** Precision bombing

**Features:**
- Analog computer for trajectory
- Gyroscopic stabilization
- Automatic bomb release
- Greatly improved accuracy

**Secrecy:** Most secret device of WWII, bombardiers sworn to protect

## MONIAC (1949)

**Creator:** Bill Phillips, London School of Economics

**Type:** Hydraulic analog computer

**Purpose:** Model British economy

**Mechanism:**
```
    [National Income Tank]
         │
    ┌────┼────┐
    │    │    │
    ▼    ▼    ▼
  [Tax] [Savings] [Spending]
    │    │    │
    └────┼────┘
         │
         ▼
    [Flow meters show economic variables]
```

**Variables Represented:**
- Income
- Taxes
- Spending
- Imports/Exports
- Interest rates
- Savings

**Significance:** Demonstrated economic principles visually; ~14 built worldwide

## FERMIAC (1947)

**Creator:** Enrico Fermi

**Type:** Monte Carlo analog computer

**Purpose:** Neutron transport calculations

**Mechanism:**
- Random number generation (dice/drum)
- Mechanical position tracking
- Statistical sampling device

**Application:** Nuclear reactor design, Manhattan Project follow-on

## Electronic Analog Computer Giants

### REAC (1946)

**Developer:** Reeves Instrument Company

**Significance:** First commercially available electronic analog computer

**Users:** Military, aerospace, universities

### EAI PACE TR-48 (1963)

**Features:**
- Solid-state (transistor)
- 24 integrators
- Modular design
- Portable (desktop)

**Impact:** Widely used in education and industry

### EAI 231R (1963)

**Scale:** Large scientific installation

**Specifications:**
- 120+ amplifiers
- 0.01% accuracy
- Expandable architecture
- Research-grade precision

**Applications:**
- Aerospace
- Nuclear engineering
- Process simulation

### EAI 8800 (1967)

**Type:** Hybrid analog-digital

**Features:**
- Integrated PDP-8 digital computer
- Automatic patching
- Digital coefficient setting
- Advanced data acquisition

**Significance:** Premier hybrid system of its era

## Specialized Famous Machines

### Link Trainer (1934-1970s)

**Creator:** Edwin Link

**Type:** Flight simulator

**Features:**
- Analog flight dynamics
- Motion platform
- Instrument simulation
- Realistic cockpit

**Impact:** Trained >500,000 pilots during WWII

### Typhoon (1947)

**Developer:** MIT Servomechanisms Lab

**Application:** Real-time flight simulation

**Significance:** Early aerospace analog computing

### TRIDAC (1954)

**Location:** Royal Aircraft Establishment, UK

**Purpose:** Flight simulation, particularly supersonic

**Features:**
- Massive installation
- Multiple rooms of equipment
- Real-time operation

## Legacy Machines

### Heathkit EC-1 (1960)

**Type:** Educational analog computer kit

**Features:**
- Build-it-yourself
- 9 op-amps
- Low cost ($199.95 kit)
- Learning tool

**Significance:** Brought analog computing to hobbyists and students

### Comdyna GP-6 (1970s-present)

**Type:** Educational desktop

**Features:**
- Solid-state
- Patch panel
- Modern production
- Still available for education

## Modern Famous Systems

### THE Analog Thing (THAT) (2021)[^2]

**Developer:** Anabrid GmbH (Germany)

**Features:**
- Open-source design
- Low cost (~$300)
- Educational focus
- Modern components

**Significance:** Revival of interest in analog computing

### Mythic Analog AI Accelerator (2020s)

**Type:** Analog neural network processor

**Application:** AI inference

**Significance:** Demonstrates analog computing's future in machine learning
